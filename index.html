Q&A

Web 1.0 is the earliest version, characterized by static pages, limited interactivity, and one-way communication. Mostly read-only and very centralized.

Web 2.0 introduced user-generated content, interactivity. Allowed both read and write, less centralized, and was very social focused.

Web 3.0 focused on users owning their data using blockchain and smart contracts, uses various elements of AI.

The main 3 differences between Web1 and Web2 are:

  1. Interaction - Web1 was read only where Web2 allowed both read and write.
  2. Control and Ownership - in Web1 the websites were owned by individuals or companies. In Web2 most data and content are controlled by big Platforms such as google, facebook, youtube and more.
  3. Technology - Web1 had mostly static pages with html and css, while Web2 allowed dynamic pages using JavaScript, APIs and more.

The main 3 differences between Web2 and Web3 are:

  1. Interaction - Web3 has more then Read-write, it also focuses on execute using AI-driven interactions
  2. Control and Ownership - Web3 is much more decentralized, and mostly controlled by users (blockchain-based)
  3. Technology - Web3 incorporates more statistical domains, such as AI, machine learning and smart contracts.

Now lets say we search for "Why do programmers love coffee so much? ☕💻".

  1. In Web1 we will probably use AltaVista as our search engine, and get a few static HTML pages. We wont be able to interact with them at all.
  2. In Web2 we will use Google as our search engine. we get a mix of results from probably reddit, youtube and more big platforms. we could comment in the results pages themselves and interact with other pepole who arrived to the same page.
  3. In Web3 we might choose to use an LLM such as ChatGPT to get a result. the LLM will understand my intent and would probably give me a context-aware answer. Also, the LLM might give me personlized answer based on our previous interactions.
